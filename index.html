How I work

Design as an argument, not decoration.

Restraint over effects. If something on the slide isn’t doing work, it goes.

100%

Bespoke systems

Every deck gets its own type and colour. I don’t reuse a template.

Narrative-first

Structure first. One idea per slide, then I make it look good.

Data, legible

Charts you can read from the back of the room.

Brand systems

I leave files your team can actually use. Type, colours and templates, documented.

Built with teams

I work through the messy review rounds, not around them.

A good deck is half editing, half design. I do both.

I make presentations for companies in finance, pharma, industry, consumer goods and software. Usually high-stakes, usually on a deadline.

I care a lot about hierarchy and about what gets cut. That’s usually what separates a deck that informs from one that convinces.

Every project here, I art-directed, wrote and built myself. Five of them, on purpose, so they don’t all look the same.
